Impacts and Outcomes of Air Pollution

  • Air pollution is a risk for all-cause mortality as well as specific diseases.

    • The specific diseases are related to the exposure to air pollution including stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, pneumonia, and cataract.

    In 2019, 99% of the world population was living in places where the WHO's strictest 2021 air quality quideline levels were not met

    Around 4 million people died in 2019 from exposure to fine particulate outdoor air pollution, with the highest death rates occurring in East Asia and Central Europe.

    Additionally the exposure to air pollution also indicates increased risks for adverse pregnancy outcomes.
